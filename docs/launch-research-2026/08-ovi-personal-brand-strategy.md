## North star

**When someone in Sri Lanka's tech/startup/business scene hears "Ovi" → they think Dinaya.lk.**  
**When they see Dinaya.lk → they think of a 19-year-old founder who is actually building.**

Everything filters through that single outcome. Personal brand is not separate from GTM — it is the **credibility layer** that makes salon owners, collaborators, and future investors take the outreach seriously.

---

## Positioning

> Ovi is a 19-year-old Sri Lankan founder building Dinaya.lk — booking and business management for local service businesses. He's not just a developer. He's digitizing how Sri Lankan SMBs take appointments, one booking page at a time — from a competition win to first real clients in Colombo.

### One-liners (pick by surface)

| Surface | Line |
|---------|------|
| LinkedIn headline | Building Dinaya.lk — booking SaaS for Sri Lankan SMBs \| Co-founder @ Ardeno Studio |
| Bio / short | Building Dinaya.lk — booking SaaS for Sri Lankan SMBs \| 3rd place @ tryitout.io |
| Elevator (spoken) | We give WhatsApp-heavy businesses a real booking link — book, pay deposits, get reminders — without replacing how they already talk to customers. |

### What Dinaya promise to echo (never contradict)

From brand voice — personal posts should reinforce, not reinvent:

- Sell a **finished booking page**, not "software" or "AI platform"
- **WhatsApp complement** — "gives WhatsApp a booking link"
- **Local** — LKR, PayHere, Colombo salons, Sri Lankan habits
- **Show proof** — live `{slug}.dinaya.lk`, dashboard screenshots, first booking (with consent)

## Audience & channel split

Personal brand ≠ only LinkedIn. Split channels by job:

| Channel | Primary audience | Job of content |
|---------|------------------|----------------|
| **LinkedIn** | SL tech/startup, builders, investors, accelerators | Credibility, narrative, hiring/collab inbound |
| **Instagram** | Salon owners, local SMBs, creators | Product proof, pain hooks, DM "DINAYA" |
| **WhatsApp / in-person** | Warm leads from both | Close via live setup — see [07 First 100](07-first-100-customers-action-plan.md) |

**Rule:** LinkedIn builds trust with the ecosystem. Instagram converts salon owners. Do not expect LinkedIn posts alone to fill the booking pipeline — every LinkedIn post should still **show product proof** so SMB owners who look you up see something real.

## Content pillars (mapped to Dinaya GTM)

| Pillar | % of LinkedIn | Example angles | Proof artifact required |
|--------|---------------|----------------|-------------------------|
| **1. Building in public** | 40% | Ship log, onboarding story, booking count | Screenshot or live slug |
| **2. Founder reality** | 25% | Exams + building, sales friction, almost quit | Honest, no hustle porn |
| **3. SL tech scene POV** | 20% | WhatsApp booking culture, builder events | Opinion + one Dinaya tie-in |
| **4. Craft & learnings** | 15% | Subdomains, PayHere, Next.js decisions | Technical screenshot or snippet |

### What NOT to associate with

- Random hot takes (React vs Vue, AI hype) unrelated to your story
- Other project announcements (NudgeBox, Octane, Ceyfi) — Dinaya is the main character
- Motivational fluff — "wake up and grind"
- Inconsistent narrative — too many unrelated topics
- Unrelated complaining — keep professional or product-related
- **Banned in all Dinaya-adjacent copy:** "AI platform" as lead, dinaya.app, unproven "guaranteed X% bookings", voice receptionist "live for everyone"

## Tone & voice

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| Direct and specific | Vague startup speak |
| Show the work (screenshots, demos, numbers) | Tell without showing |
| Honest about what's hard | Fake positivity |
| Confident but not arrogant | "We're disrupting X" |
| Sri Lankan context, global quality | Overly regional or overly Western |
| Short and punchy | Long essays nobody reads |

**Personality:** The 19-year-old founder who's actually shipping, not just talking about it.

## Launch post — retrospective (tryitout.io)

**Post this first.** Pin to Featured once live.

### Ship-ready draft

```
About a month ago, I submitted Dinaya.lk to a competition.

I wasn't sure it was ready.

It's a booking platform I've been building for Sri Lankan service businesses — salons, cafes, restaurants — the kinds of places still taking appointments through WhatsApp and phone calls in 2025.

We placed 3rd at tryitout.io.

I didn't post about it at the time. Honestly, I wasn't sure what came next.

Now I do.

I'm going all in on Dinaya.

We just onboarded our first real client — a salon in Colombo. Custom booking page at {slug}.dinaya.lk, PayHere deposits, reminders — the full setup.

The goal: make Dinaya the default booking infrastructure for Sri Lankan SMBs.

If you run a local service business in Sri Lanka — or know someone who does — I'd love to talk. Comment or DM.

dinaya.lk
```

- [ ] Replace `{slug}.dinaya.lk` with the actual live slug (or link directly)

- [ ] Visual: carousel with (1) tryitout result, (2) booking page on mobile, (3) dashboard calendar

## 30-day LinkedIn calendar (post-launch)

**Cadence:** 1 post/week minimum on LinkedIn. Cross-post product demos to Instagram same week (adapt hook for salon owners — see [03 Content Calendar](03-content-calendar.md)).

### Week 1 — Launch the narrative
**Post:** Retrospective (above).  
**IG same week:** Pain reel — "Bookings in Sri Lanka are still stuck in DMs" (from content calendar T-21).

### Week 2 — Show the product

**LinkedIn draft:**
```
Most booking tools assume your customers will download an app or create an account.

Sri Lankan service businesses don't work like that.

Here's what a Dinaya booking page actually looks like — 30 seconds, mobile, no app:

[Screen recording: pick service → pick time → confirm → optional PayHere deposit]

This is what we're shipping for salons and clinics in Colombo first.

If your Instagram bio still says "DM to book" — this is the upgrade.

dinaya.lk
```

**Attach:** 30–45s screen recording, subtitles, product visible in first 3 seconds.

### Week 3 — Client story

**LinkedIn draft:**
```
Onboarding our first salon client on Dinaya took longer than I expected — and taught me more than any feature spec.

What actually happened:

1. They sent their service menu on WhatsApp (PDF + voice notes)
2. We built the booking page — services, staff, hours — in one sitting
3. They pasted the link in their Instagram bio before we hung up
4. First booking came in [within X hours / next day — use real number]

The hard part wasn't the software. It was convincing the owner that customers would actually use a link instead of DMing.

They did.

[Optional: tag client if they agreed]

Building for Sri Lankan SMBs means meeting them where they already are — WhatsApp, Instagram — and giving that workflow a booking link.

Know a salon or clinic still running on DMs? Intro welcome.
```

**Permission:** Get written OK before naming the business or tagging.

### Week 4 — Builder angle

**LinkedIn draft:**
```
Technical decision we made early on Dinaya: every business gets {slug}.dinaya.lk on signup.

Sounds simple. Here's why it mattered:

→ Salon owners understand "your link" immediately
→ No DNS confusion during onboarding
→ Custom domains (Growth plan) sit on top — not instead of

Stack: Next.js App Router + Vercel wildcard domains + middleware rewrite to /book/[slug]

Biggest mistake I almost made: making businesses configure domains before their first booking. Nobody cares about DNS on day one. They care if a customer can book tonight.

If you're building for non-technical SMBs — optimize for "live in 20 minutes," not architectural purity.

[Optional: architecture diagram or middleware snippet screenshot]
```

## Long game

The brand isn't the posts. The brand is what the posts prove you are: someone who showed up, shipped, and built something real in Sri Lanka.

That trail matters for:

- **Customer trust** — SMBs checking you out before signing up
- **Investor credibility** — YC application, future rounds
- **Network growth** — Sri Lankan and SEA tech ecosystem
- **Your own clarity** — knowing what you're building and why
