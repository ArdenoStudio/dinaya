# Dinaya Launch Research 2026

Last updated: 2026-06-03, Asia/Colombo.

This folder is a text-only launch pack for Dinaya. It covers current Sri Lankan market context, launch sequencing, content, direct outreach, promo/referral/influencer strategy, and product gaps to keep in mind. No application code was changed for this research pack.

## Executive Verdict

Launch Dinaya as a concierge-led Sri Lanka booking and growth platform for service businesses, not as a generic scheduling SaaS.

The strongest wedge is appointment-heavy local businesses that already sell through Instagram, Facebook, WhatsApp, and phone calls but lose time to manual coordination. Start with beauty and wellness because the pain is visible, the content is naturally visual, and competitors already prove demand. Expand into clinics, consultants, fitness, tutoring, auto services, and professional services after the first proof points.

Dinaya should lead with outcomes:

- "Stop losing bookings in DMs."
- "Let customers book, pay deposits, and get reminders from one link."
- "Fill quiet slots with deals instead of guessing."
- "Track bookings, revenue, staff, reviews, and campaigns in one dashboard."

AI should be positioned as the growth layer after businesses trust the core booking workflow. AI Voice Receptionist should stay "coming soon" or "managed preview" until it is actually rolled out.

## First 14 Days

1. Pick one beachhead: salons, barbers, spas, nail studios, makeup artists, and wellness clinics in Colombo, Gampaha, Kandy, and Galle.
2. Build a lead sheet with 150 businesses from Instagram, Google Maps, Facebook, and referrals.
3. DM or WhatsApp 20 businesses per day with a free concierge setup offer.
4. Set up 10 pilot booking pages manually and get each pilot to share their link/QR.
5. Post 5 to 7 short videos per week: pain skits, demo clips, founder build-in-public, and pilot stories.
6. Use existing referral links, UTM links, and Dinaya Deals for campaign tracking until true promo codes are built.
7. Recruit 5 founder partners from real service businesses, not generic lifestyle influencers.
