# 02 Launch Plan

Last updated: 2026-06-03, Asia/Colombo.

## Goal

Dinaya's first launch goal should be proof, not hype.

Target for the first 90 days:

- 50 qualified businesses onboarded.
- 25 businesses taking at least one real booking through Dinaya.
- 10 businesses taking 10+ bookings in a month.
- 5 public case studies/testimonials.
- 3 creator or agency partners sending qualified leads.
- A repeatable playbook for one beachhead vertical.

North star:

> Active businesses receiving real weekly bookings through Dinaya.

## Launch Position

Use this as the main launch message:

> Dinaya helps Sri Lankan service businesses turn DMs, calls, and social traffic into confirmed bookings.

The product story:

1. Share a booking link.
2. Customers choose service, staff, location, date, and time.
3. The business collects deposits when needed.
4. Dinaya sends reminders and keeps the dashboard updated.
5. Pro and Growth tools help the business fill quiet slots, collect reviews, and rebook customers.

## Beachhead Offer

Offer name:

> Dinaya Founder Setup

Offer:

- Free booking page setup.
- Free service/staff/location import.
- Free QR code and WhatsApp share copy.
- 60 days Pro for the first 50 qualifying businesses.
- Founder badge/testimonial opportunity.

Qualification rules:

- Appointment-based service business.
- Has at least one active social page or Google Business profile.
- Has at least 2 services with prices/durations.
- Owner agrees to share the Dinaya link publicly within 7 days.
- Owner agrees to one feedback call or testimonial if the product helps.

## 90-Day Sequence

### Days 1-14: Pre-Launch Proof

Objectives:

- Build the launch lead sheet.
- Get 10 pilot businesses set up.
- Create demo material from real workflows.
- Publish coming-soon and pain-point content.

Actions:

- Create lead sheet with 150 businesses.
- DM/WhatsApp 20 leads per day.
- Offer concierge setup, not "book a product demo."
- Build 3 demo businesses if live pilots are slow: salon, clinic, fitness studio.
- Record screen clips: booking page, choosing a slot, dashboard, Deals, reminders, reports.
- Publish 5 to 7 short posts per week.
- Ask every friendly founder, designer, marketer, and local business owner for 3 introductions.

### Days 15-30: Public Launch

Objectives:

- Announce Dinaya clearly.
- Convert replies into demos.
- Start founder partner/influencer pipeline.
- Push the Founder 50 offer.

Actions:

- Publish main launch video.
- Publish a carousel explaining "how Dinaya works in 5 steps."
- Publish 3 vertical-specific reels: salon, clinic, fitness.
- Run a live setup session on Instagram or LinkedIn.
- Start small paid tests only after organic hooks show replies.
- Collect and publish pilot proof, even if small: "First booking came through in 12 minutes" beats vague hype.

### Days 31-60: Conversion and Proof

Objectives:

- Convert active pilots to paid plans.
- Turn best users into case studies.
- Tighten onboarding.
- Find the repeatable segment.

Actions:

- Run weekly onboarding clinics.
- Set up a standard 30-minute business import process.
- Track activation: booking page live, first customer booking, first payment/deposit, first reminder, first repeat booking.
- Ask every onboarded business to post their booking QR/link.
- Create 2 case study videos: owner problem, Dinaya setup, first result.
- Start creator/agency partner program with tracked links.

### Days 61-90: Scale the Winning Segment

Objectives:

- Double down on the vertical/city that converts best.
- Introduce annual plans and referral incentives.
- Prepare the promo-code build if launch demand proves it.

Actions:

- Choose one winning segment from actual conversion data.
- Build a niche landing narrative: "Dinaya for salons", "Dinaya for clinics", or similar.
- Partner with 3 niche creators or local business pages.
- Run one city/vertical campaign: "Colombo salon booking week" or "Kandy wellness booking week."
- Scope promo-code feature only after manual code demand is proven.

## Onboarding Workflow

Promise:

> Send us your services, prices, staff, and opening hours. We will set up your booking page.

Business provides:

- Business name
- Logo/photo
- Category and city
- Services, durations, prices
- Staff names
- Opening hours
- Payment/deposit rules
- Cancellation policy
- WhatsApp/phone
- Instagram/Facebook links

Dinaya setup checklist:

- Create business profile.
- Add services, staff, locations, and availability.
- Add PayHere if ready.
- Turn on reminders where available.
- Generate booking URL and QR.
- Add referral/UTM tracking for the channel.
- Send owner a launch message they can post.

## Metrics

Weekly acquisition metrics:

- Leads added.
- Leads contacted.
- Replies.
- Demo/setup calls booked.
- Setup calls completed.
- Businesses onboarded.
- Booking pages shared publicly.
- First bookings.
- Paid conversions.

Activation metrics:

- Time from first contact to live booking page.
- Time from live booking page to first customer booking.
- Number of bookings per active business in first 14 days.
- Deposit/payment adoption.
- Reminder usage.
- Deals usage.

Content metrics:

- Profile visits.
- Link clicks.
- DMs from business owners.
- Saves and shares on educational posts.
- Cost per qualified DM if running ads.

Partner metrics:

- Creator link clicks.
- Qualified referrals.
- Activated businesses.
- Paid conversions.
- Payout per paid conversion.

## Budget

Bootstrap version:

- LKR 0-25,000/month on ads.
- Focus on founder content, direct DMs, WhatsApp, referrals, and free setup.
- Use free/cheap creator collaborations with revenue share.

Test budget version:

- LKR 50,000-150,000/month for 30 days.
- 70 percent to retargeting and best-performing reels.
- 20 percent to creator UGC.
- 10 percent to lead-form tests.

Do not spend heavily before three organic hooks produce real DMs from business owners.
